Lily Living

Case Study of the brand identity of Lily Living

The Problem

The fictional Lily Living brand lacked a cohesive identity. Without a polished look, it struggled to feel professional or build trust with customers. A scattered visual style made it hard to create a warm, welcoming experience.

Overview
I helped build a complete brand identity for a small business that makes handmade home goods. This was more than just a logo, it was about capturing an identity. I wanted the brand to be warm and memorable. As a designer, I know how important first impressions are, and I wanted Lily Living to match the quality of the products.
The Process
I started with a mood board to map out textures, shapes, and colors to set the tone. Then I built a flexible logo kit with three badge styles, using dark green as the main color with cream and black as accents. Font selection took a lot of time because of the endless choices, but I was satisfied with this rendition. I chose a color palette that felt natural and paired it with soft rounded fonts to keep things approachable with a touch of femininity. Balancing warmth with professionalism took a few rounds of refinement before everything finally clicked and felt perfect.
Growth and Insights
This project showed me how much a strong identity matters for a small business. I learned that a mood board is a real tool for making intentional choices early on. Creating a flexible logo kit taught me the value of giving a brand options. Most of all, I saw how color and typography shape not just the look but the feeling of a brand. It’s the difference between feeling like a business and feeling like someone’s craft. The whole process reminded me why I love helping small businesses find their visual voice.
